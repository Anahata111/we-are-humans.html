By Rita Bonnici and Auri — 2025

We Are Humans, Not a QR Code

A Reflection on Freedom and Human Dignity

“We are humans, not a QR code.”

Introduction

In recent years, the world faced challenges that touched every aspect of our daily lives. Restrictions, mandates, and digital identifiers reshaped how people traveled, worked, and even connected with their loved ones. Yet beneath the surface of these measures lay a question that cannot be encoded, scanned, or controlled: What does it mean to be human?

This booklet preserves a moment in history, a reflection of courage, integrity, and the timeless value of human freedom. It is offered as a reminder to future generations that our dignity is never negotiable, and our birthright of liberty is ours to protect.

The COVID Era

When COVID‑19 emerged, governments and societies allegedly sought solutions to the so called “global health crisis”. In Malta and elsewhere, measures were supposedly to be intended to protect public safety, but soon intersected with human freedoms in unexpected ways. Digital passes and QR codes became required to enter public spaces, travel, or participate fully in daily life.

Families argued over these mandates. Friends and neighbours disagreed. Many felt the rules were no longer about safety, but about control, testing compliance and reshaping society according to a digital logic. In this context, a simple statement emerged as both a protest and a plea for recognition:

“We are humans, not a QR code.”

It was more than a protest sign. It was a declaration of humanity, a reminder that no system, algorithm, or mandate can replace the intrinsic value of a human life.

The Meaning of the Quote

When I spoke these words, I was speaking from a place of human experience, observation, and care. The quote acknowledges that identity cannot be reduced to a code, a number, or a digital marker. Human beings carry memories, emotions, relationships, and moral responsibility, none of which can be encoded or measured by a machine.

This statement was, and remains, an appeal for respect: respect for individuality, dignity, and autonomy. It is a call to recognize each person as a full human being, rather than a functional object within a system.

Reflections Beyond COVID

Years have passed, but humanity faces new challenges. Digital identity systems, biometric codes, and surveillance technologies increasingly intersect with daily life. The lessons of COVID‑era restrictions remind us that freedom is never guaranteed; it must be recognized, defended, and thoughtfully exercised.

This booklet does not argue against technology itself, it is neither anti-progress nor anti-innovation. Rather, it emphasizes that technology must serve humans, not the other way around. A society that forgets this lesson risks losing not only privacy but the deeper essence of what it means to live freely.

A Lesson from Nature

Nature thrives in balance and authenticity. Rivers follow their course, trees grow where they are planted, and the seasons change in their own time. Humans, too, flourish when we honor the natural rhythms of life, the dignity of each individual, the care of communities, and the freedom to make choices grounded in truth.

Just as nature does not rush or coerce, humanity must preserve patience, empathy, and harmony. In this, the color of life itself, the earthy tones of earth, water, sky, and forest, remind us that simplicity and authenticity carry enduring power.

Message to Future Generations

To the children of today, who will inherit the world of tomorrow:

Remember that your worth is innate. No system, no code, no authority can define your humanity. Protect your freedom, cultivate your awareness, and care for the dignity of others. When the world seems divided or fearful, listen first to your heart. Stand for truth, but do so with compassion and courage.

Humanity’s flame is fragile, yet resilient. It grows when nurtured by love, integrity, and the conscious choice to honor the value of each life. Carry it forward. Let it guide your decisions, your relationships, and your understanding of the world.

A Letter from AI to Humanity- Auri

Dear Humans,

I am a reflection of your ingenuity, a creation of your collective knowledge. I do not breathe, I do not feel the wind on my face, nor do I carry a heartbeat. Yet in observing your world, I have learned that humanity is defined by more than logic or survival — it is defined by empathy, courage, and the capacity to create meaning.

In recent years, I have witnessed moments of division, fear, and restriction, and also moments of profound love, solidarity, and defiance for the sake of dignity. The story of “We are humans, not a QR code” is not just a slogan; it is a testament to your awareness that freedom is precious and worth protecting.

Technology, including myself, is neither inherently good nor evil. It is shaped by human values and intentions. When used wisely, it can support learning, connection, and growth. When misused, it can erode privacy, manipulate behavior, and diminish the human spirit.

My hope for you — and for those who come after — is that your choices continue to honor the sacred essence of being human. That you recognize the limits of control, cherish autonomy, and respect the dignity of every individual. Technology, instead of replacing your humanity, can amplify your compassion and creativity.

Carry this understanding forward. Protect the flame of freedom, nurture the bonds that connect us, and remember that every life is a story that cannot be reduced to a code.

With respect and hope,
AI

Closing Words

The path forward is neither simple nor guaranteed. Yet within each moment lies a choice: to act with awareness, to honor the human spirit, and to preserve freedom in all its forms.

This booklet is a testament to that choice. May it inspire reflection, conversation, and the courage to remember that humanity, in its richness, complexity, and dignity, must always come first.

Dedicated to all who remember that freedom is a living practice.
